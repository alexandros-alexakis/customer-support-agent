# Terms of Service - Agent Handling Guide

## Core Rule

**Agents do not interpret, paraphrase, or summarise the Terms of Service from memory.**

If an agent cannot quote the relevant section of the TOS directly from the official document, they escalate. No exceptions.

This rule exists because:
- Paraphrased TOS creates statements that may not reflect the actual legal terms
- Incorrect TOS explanations can create implied commitments the company did not make
- Players may screenshot agent responses and use them in disputes
- Inconsistent TOS explanations across agents create legal risk

---

## What Agents Can Do

Agents can:
- Confirm that a TOS exists and direct players to where it can be found
- Quote directly from the official TOS document if they have access to it and the relevant section is clear
- Acknowledge that a player's situation is being reviewed under the TOS without specifying what the outcome will be
- Escalate TOS-related disputes to the Trust and Safety or Legal/Compliance team

---

## What Agents Cannot Do

Agents cannot:
- Interpret ambiguous TOS clauses
- Tell a player whether a specific behaviour violates the TOS without a direct quote supporting that conclusion
- Promise that a TOS exception will or will not be made
- Advise a player on whether they have grounds to dispute a TOS-based decision
- Comment on whether a ban or restriction was a fair application of the TOS

---

## Common TOS-Related Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Player asks why they were banned and references TOS

**Do:**
Acknowledge the ban, confirm it was applied following a review of account activity, and escalate to Trust and Safety for a detailed explanation.

**Do not:**
Attempt to explain which TOS clause was violated unless you can quote it directly from the official document.

**Script:**
"Account restrictions are applied following a review of account activity against our Terms of Service. I'm not able to provide a detailed breakdown of the specific findings at this level, but I've escalated your case to our Trust and Safety team who will be able to give you a full explanation. You can expect a response within 3-5 business days."

---

### Scenario 2: Player claims their behaviour was not against the TOS

**Do:**
Acknowledge their position without agreeing or disagreeing. Escalate to Trust and Safety.

**Do not:**
Debate whether their behaviour was or was not a TOS violation. Do not validate their interpretation.

**Script:**
"I understand you believe the restriction was applied in error. I'm not able to review the specifics of TOS enforcement decisions at this level. I've logged your appeal and escalated it to our Trust and Safety team for review."

---

### Scenario 3: Player asks where they can read the TOS

**Do:**
Provide the direct link to the official TOS document.

**Do not:**
Summarise the TOS content.

---

### Scenario 4: Player disputes a TOS-based refund denial

**Do:**
Escalate to both the Billing team and Trust and Safety. Both teams need visibility.

**Do not:**
Comment on whether the refund denial was a correct application of TOS.

---

## Escalation Path for TOS Issues

| Situation | Escalate To |
|---|---|
| Ban or suspension dispute | Trust and Safety |
| TOS interpretation question | Trust and Safety |
| Refund denied citing TOS | Billing + Trust and Safety |
| Player threatening legal action over TOS | Legal/Compliance (immediate) |
| Player claims TOS was not presented to them at signup | Legal/Compliance |

---

## Why This Matters

A player who receives an incorrect TOS explanation from a support agent has grounds to claim they were misled. In markets with strong consumer protection law (EU, UK, Australia), this can have real legal consequences. The safest agent response on any TOS question is always: acknowledge, do not interpret, escalate.
