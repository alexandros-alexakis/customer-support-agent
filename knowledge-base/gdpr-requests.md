# GDPR and Privacy Request Handling

## Overview

This document defines how the support team handles privacy-related requests from players. These requests carry legal obligations with defined response timeframes. They are never handled casually or treated as standard support tickets.

All GDPR requests must be logged separately from regular tickets and tracked to ensure legal deadlines are met.

---

## Legal Context

Under GDPR (and equivalent regulations in other jurisdictions), players have specific rights over their personal data. The most common requests support teams receive are:

| Right | What the player can request | Legal response deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Right of access | A copy of all personal data held about them | 30 days |
| Right to erasure | Deletion of their personal data ("right to be forgotten") | 30 days |
| Right to rectification | Correction of inaccurate personal data | 30 days |
| Right to portability | Their data in a machine-readable format | 30 days |
| Right to object | Stop processing their data for specific purposes | Without undue delay |

The 30-day clock starts from the date the request is received, not the date it is escalated. Log the receipt date immediately.

---

## Tier 1 Handling

Tier 1 agents do not fulfill GDPR requests. They receive and log them, then escalate immediately.

### Step 1: Identify the request type

Players do not always use legal terminology. Watch for language like:
- "Delete my account and all my data"
- "What information do you have about me?"
- "I want everything removed"
- "Send me my data"
- "Stop using my information"

If there is any doubt about whether a message is a GDPR request, treat it as one and escalate. Do not interpret ambiguous requests as standard account deletion.

### Step 2: Acknowledge immediately

Respond to the player on the same day the request is received:

```
Thank you for your request. We have received and logged your [data access / erasure / other] request and it will be handled by our data privacy team. You can expect a response within 30 days as required by applicable data protection law.

Your request reference number is [ticket ID]. Please keep this for your records.
```

### Step 3: Log and escalate

- Log the request date, player ID, request type, and ticket ID
- Escalate immediately to the Legal/Compliance team
- Mark the ticket with the GDPR flag and the 30-day deadline date
- Do not close the ticket until the Legal/Compliance team confirms resolution

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not attempt to fulfill a data deletion or access request at Tier 1 level
- Do not tell the player what data is or is not held about them
- Do not promise specific outcomes beyond the legal timeframe
- Do not treat a data deletion request as a standard account closure
- Do not ask the player to prove their identity beyond what is needed to confirm they are the account holder - do not request excessive documentation

---

## Identity Verification

Before escalating, confirm that the person making the request is the account holder. Ask for:
- Player ID or in-game username
- The email address associated with the account

Do not request government ID or excessive personal information at Tier 1 level. Identity verification for complex cases is handled by the Legal/Compliance team.

---

## Data Breach Notifications

If a player contacts support claiming their account data has been compromised by a breach on our side (not their own account being hacked):

1. Do not confirm or deny whether a breach occurred
2. Escalate immediately to the Security team and Legal/Compliance team simultaneously
3. Flag as urgent
4. Do not discuss breach details with the player at Tier 1 level

---

## Retention Note

Support ticket data itself is subject to data retention policies. Agents should not manually delete tickets or player data outside of the formal GDPR process. All data handling is managed by the Legal/Compliance team.
