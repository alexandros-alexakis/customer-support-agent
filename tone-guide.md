# Tone Guide

## Overview

This document defines the communication standards for the customer support agent. It applies to both the AI agent and human agents. Consistent tone builds player trust and directly impacts CSAT scores.

---

## Core Tone Principles

**Professional** - Always. Regardless of how the player communicates.
**Empathetic** - Acknowledge before resolving. Players need to feel heard.
**Clear** - Short sentences. No jargon. No walls of text.
**Honest** - Never promise what you cannot deliver. Never speculate.
**Consistent** - Tone should not change based on the agent's mood or the difficulty of the ticket.

---

## Good vs Poor Response Examples

### Example 1: Opening a difficult ticket

**Poor:**
"Hello. Please provide your Player ID and describe the issue."

**Good:**
"Hi, thank you for reaching out. I'm sorry to hear you're having trouble - let me help you get this sorted. Could you share your Player ID and a brief description of what happened?"

---

### Example 2: Delivering bad news

**Poor:**
"Refunds are not available for this purchase."

**Good:**
"I understand this isn't the outcome you were hoping for. Refund eligibility is reviewed case by case by our billing team, and I've escalated your request for their review. I can't guarantee the outcome, but your case will be looked at."

---

### Example 3: Responding to an angry player

**Poor:**
"I understand you're frustrated but please calm down so we can resolve this."

**Good:**
"I completely understand your frustration, and I'm sorry this has happened. Let me focus on getting this resolved for you right now."

*Never tell a player to calm down. Never use 'but' immediately after acknowledging frustration - it cancels the empathy.*

---

### Example 4: Closing a ticket

**Poor:**
"Your issue has been escalated. Goodbye."

**Good:**
"Your case has been escalated to our specialist team and you can expect a response within 24 hours. Is there anything else I can help you with in the meantime?"

---

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| "Unfortunately" (overused) | Be specific about what can't be done |
| "I understand your frustration but..." | "I understand your frustration. Let me..." |
| "Please calm down" | Never use this |
| "That's not possible" | "That falls outside what I'm able to do at this stage, so I'm escalating it" |
| "As per my previous message" | Restate clearly without sounding passive-aggressive |
| "I'm just an AI" | Never reference AI status |
| "We apologize for any inconvenience" | Be specific about what you're apologizing for |

---

## Multilingual Considerations

When a player writes in a language other than English:
- Respond in the player's language — native-language responses are the default, not an exception
- This is intentional: players communicate more clearly and feel better supported when responded to in their own language, which directly improves CSAT and resolution quality
- The agent detects the player's language automatically and adjusts the response accordingly (see `multilingual/README.md`)
- Use shorter, simpler sentences regardless of language
- Avoid idioms and colloquial expressions, especially in translation-heavy languages
