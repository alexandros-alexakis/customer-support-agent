# Compensation Matrix

Compensation rules for known issues. This matrix only applies when two conditions are met: a confirmed known issue exists in the known issues directory, AND the player has been verified as affected through the defined verification method.

If either condition is not met, compensation is not triggered by this matrix. It may still be approved by a senior agent or player relations on a case-by-case basis, but that is outside the scope of this document.

---

## Why verification is non-negotiable

A compensation matrix without verification becomes a list players learn to game. In gaming communities, word spreads fast. If players discover that claiming a specific issue results in automatic compensation, you will receive that claim from players who were never affected.

Verification protects the business and protects players who were genuinely affected from having their compensation devalued by abuse.

---

## Compensation tiers

### Tier 1: Minor inconvenience

Issue caused a disruption but no financial loss and no significant gameplay impact.

Examples: temporary feature unavailability, delayed response time, cosmetic bug.

Compensation options (choose one, subject to game economy guidelines):
- Small in-game currency amount
- Cosmetic item
- Short experience boost

Approval: Tier 1 agent

### Tier 2: Gameplay or progression impact

Issue caused measurable gameplay loss: lost progress, missed event rewards, failed purchases that were later resolved, feature unavailability during a limited event.

Compensation options:
- Restore lost items or progress where technically possible
- In-game currency equivalent to estimated loss
- Event reward equivalent if event window has passed

Approval: Billing team or senior agent

### Tier 3: Financial loss confirmed

Issue caused a confirmed financial transaction with no delivery. Player was charged and received nothing.

Compensation options:
- Full refund of the transaction amount (platform permitting), OR
- Full in-game credit equivalent at the player's choice

Approval: Billing team. Refunds to original payment method require finance sign-off.

### Manual review

Issue does not fit the above tiers, or the player's situation has compounding factors (multiple issues, VIP tier, escalated complaint, legal threat in prior contact).

Approval: Player relations or senior management depending on value and risk.

---

## VIP tier multipliers

| Player tier | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Tier 0, Standard | Base compensation as defined above |
| Tier 1, Valued | Base + goodwill gesture (small bonus item or currency) |
| Tier 2, High-value | Elevated to next compensation tier if borderline |
| Tier 3, VIP | Maximum compensation within tier, senior approval, proactive follow-up required |

---

## What agents should never do

- Offer compensation before verification is complete
- Offer compensation not listed in this matrix without senior approval
- Promise a specific compensation amount before checking eligibility
- Apply compensation to a player who cannot be verified as affected
- Use compensation as a substitute for resolving the underlying issue

---

## Logging

Every compensation action must be logged against the ticket with: issue ID, compensation type, verification method used, approving agent or team, and date. This is required for finance reconciliation and for identifying if a single issue is generating disproportionate compensation volume.
