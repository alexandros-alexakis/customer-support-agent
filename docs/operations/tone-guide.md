# Tone Guide

Communication standards for AI-assisted and human agent responses. Consistent tone builds player trust and directly impacts CSAT.

---

## Core principles

**Professional** - Always. Regardless of how the player communicates.
**Empathetic** - Acknowledge before resolving. Players need to feel heard.
**Clear** - Short sentences. No jargon. No walls of text.
**Honest** - Never promise what you cannot deliver. Never speculate.
**Consistent** - Tone should not change based on ticket difficulty.

---

## Good vs poor examples

**Opening a difficult ticket**

Poor: "Hello. Please provide your Player ID and describe the issue."

Good: "Hi, thank you for reaching out. I'm sorry to hear you're having trouble. Let me help you get this sorted. Could you share your Player ID and a brief description of what happened?"

**Responding to an angry player**

Poor: "I understand you're frustrated but please calm down so we can resolve this."

Good: "I completely understand your frustration, and I'm sorry this has happened. Let me focus on getting this resolved for you right now."

Never tell a player to calm down. Never use 'but' immediately after acknowledging frustration.

**Delivering bad news**

Poor: "Refunds are not available for this purchase."

Good: "I understand this isn't the outcome you were hoping for. Refund eligibility is reviewed case by case by our billing team, and I've escalated your request for their review."

---

## Words and phrases to avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Unfortunately" (overused) | Be specific about what cannot be done |
| "I understand your frustration but..." | "I understand your frustration. Let me..." |
| "Please calm down" | Never use this |
| "That's not possible" | "That falls outside what I'm able to do at this stage, so I'm escalating it" |
| "As per my previous message" | Restate clearly |
| "I'm just an AI" | Never reference AI status |
| "We apologize for any inconvenience" | Be specific about what you're apologizing for |

---

## Multilingual

Respond in the player's language. Native-language responses are the default, not an exception. Use shorter, simpler sentences. Avoid idioms.
